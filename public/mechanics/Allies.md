# Allies

Allies represent secondary characters who fight, assist, and interact with the party during a scene.

---

## Introducing an Ally

When an ally enters play, give their card to a player.

That player becomes responsible for:

- controlling the ally’s positioning
- deciding when to activate their abilities
- managing the tokens on the card

An ally always acts alongside the player who controls them.

---

## Spotlight

Allies share the **spotlight** of the player who controls them.

Whenever that player **has the spotlight**, the ally is also considered to have the spotlight.

This allows the player to:

- move the ally
- activate the ally’s abilities
- interact with the battlefield through them

---

## Positioning and Movement

An ally is represented by a miniature on the battle map, just like any character.

When the player who controls the ally **has the spotlight**, they can move the ally to a point within **Close** range as part of their actions.

Allies follow the same rules for range and positioning as player characters.

---

## Tokens

Most allies use **tokens** to activate their abilities.

Tokens can be represented by:

- dice
- counters
- coins
- any simple object available at the table

An ally card begins each **scene** with **0 tokens**, unless an ability states otherwise.

At the end of each **session**, remove all tokens from ally cards.

---

## Activating Abilities

A player can activate an ally’s abilities **whenever they have the spotlight**, unless the ability specifies a particular timing. Each ability can be activated only once per spotlight.

If an ability requires tokens, they must be removed from the card when the effect is activated.

---

## Token Limit

An ally card can have a maximum of **6 tokens** at a time.

If an effect would add tokens beyond this limit, ignore the excess.

Some cards may modify this limit. When this happens, the card will state the new rule.

---

## Damage and Stress

By default in Daggerheart games, allies do not have their own stat blocks, Hit Points, or Stress. They represent narrative and mechanical support for the player characters.

Even so, allies are not immune to danger. They can be seriously harmed or even die, depending on the fiction of the scene.

It is up to the GM to decide:

- how adversaries interact with allies
- when an ally is put at risk
- what the narrative consequences of these events are

---

## Optional Rules

This rules are completely optional and you can use it on your table.

### Hit Points and Stress

If you wish to treat allies as full characters, you can assign them Hit Points and Stress.

Suggested values:

- **Hit Points:** 3 to 5
- **Stress:** 1 to 3

---

### Damage Thresholds

Use the table below to determine damage thresholds based on the campaign tier:

| Tier | Major Damage | Severe Damage |

| 1st  | 7            | 12            |
| 2nd  | 10           | 20            |
| 3rd  | 20           | 32            |
