# The Golden Skull

A gleaming golden skull, set with precious gems, rests atop a pedestal. Its surface reflects light… and something else watching from within.

## Purpose

Harass and distract targets, applying persistent pressure until they resolve the curse.

## Features

### Gilded Curse

#### Trigger

When a PC touches the skull

#### Effect

The skull animates, floating beside the PC and following them relentlessly. It constantly speaks, mocking and distracting its target. When the affected PC makes a roll, they must mark 1 Stress or make the roll with disadvantage. The skull is immune to damage and cannot be physically removed by conventional means.

### Insatiable Tribute

#### Trigger

When the skull is offered gold

#### Effect

The skull eagerly consumes wealth. If the party offers at least 2 Chests of Gold, the skull is satisfied. It lets out a final remark before vanishing.

## Design notes

This hazard creates persistent psychological pressure rather than immediate danger. I took this concept from Yaka, an NPC from the [Tomb of Annihilation](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/toa "Tomb of Annihilation") Campaign, from the *5th Edition*. It's a funny concept to explore.
